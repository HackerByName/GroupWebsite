Communication

The field of computing has many different applications, many of which have many great contributions to society. However, one application that I think is extremely beneficial to society is communication. Social Media corporations, such as Facebook, Instagram and Twitter often get plenty of bad press for different issues, but without them and all the other types of technology-driven communication we would live very different lives.

Computing helps us to:

Keep In Touch
  • With free secure messaging and group video calls over the internet, keeping in touch with people all over the world is easier than ever before.
  • Businesses are able to expand their reach into new markets in different areas or countries.
  • Workers have the ability to be much more flexible, even working from home without the need to commute to the office.
    		•

    Keep Up To Date
  • Over half of all adults in America use Facebook as a source of news.
  • In a study of US students, only 5% received news from Newspapers daily, compared with 72% getting daily news from social media.
  • The usage of the internet to provide news means that far more people can be kept up to date on important issues, almost instantly.
  • As well as finding out what's going on on a global scale, technological advances have made it much easier to find out what is going on within people's own friendship groups and families.
    		•

    Keep Safe
  • With advances in technology come new ways to keep us and our data safe.
  • As communication becomes easier, it is also easier to inform people of the best practices to keep safe in many situations.
  • While communicating online, you benefit from encryption of your messages and AI algorithms scanning other people's posts/messages for dodgy content.
    		•

    Keep Connected
  • Making connections with people is made very easy on SM due to algorithms that recommend people to each other based on different criteria.
  • This helps people to make new friends with people who have similar interests or easily find people they have met in person.
  • This creates large, interconnected friendship groups that result in more social interaction between people.
